Relative Motion in a Current-Carrying Wire
This version makes the visible number of electrons equal to the visible number of positive ions so the neutrality idea is easier to teach. It also lets a moving external test charge bend away from the wire with a clearly parabolic-looking path. A highlighted measurement segment counts charges in the same part of the wire. In the lab frame the counts match, while in the electron frame the ion lattice is drawn moving upward with conceptual length contraction, so more positive charge can fit into the same segment.
